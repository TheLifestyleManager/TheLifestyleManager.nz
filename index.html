Services

Every client is different. We start with where your life feels most congested and build from there. Below are the core ways I support people.

Life admin & errand support

The small tasks that pile up and weigh on your mind – I handle them. This can include booking appointments, organising tradespeople, managing renewals and subscriptions, coordinating deliveries, researching options, and staying on top of the details that keep slipping through the cracks.

You get fewer open loops, fewer “I really should do that” thoughts, and more mental space for the work, relationships and projects that actually matter to you.

Home & project coordination

Renovation coming up? Guests arriving? Holiday home to keep in order? I coordinate the moving pieces: trades, cleaners, gardeners, organisers and suppliers. I can create simple systems for the household so things don’t depend on you remembering everything.

Think of it as having someone in your corner who treats your home and projects with the same care and attention to detail you would – without you needing to be the project manager.

Travel & transitions

Travel and life transitions can be exciting – and exhausting. I plan and book travel, connect the logistics, and handle the admin around moves, new roles, relocations, school changes, or family arriving from overseas.

From itineraries and ground transport to packing lists and bookings, the aim is a smooth experience with fewer surprises, so you can focus on the moments rather than the mechanics.

Tech & systems support

Devices, apps and online accounts are supposed to make life easier. When they don’t, I step in as a patient, human guide. I help you set things up, streamline tools you already use, and connect the tech to your actual life so it works for you, not the other way around.

This can look like sorting phones and laptops, setting up shared calendars, simplifying digital filing, or helping you and your family feel more confident with the tools you already have.

Discreet personal & family support

Some support doesn’t fit neatly in a category. You might need help coordinating care for a parent, supporting a partner behind the scenes, or holding together the logistics of a busy household while you move through a demanding season.

My role is to be a calm, trusted constant in the background – someone who understands the dynamics, respects privacy, and quietly keeps things moving in the direction you want.

Frequently asked questions

What exactly does a Lifestyle Manager do?

In simple terms: I help your life run more smoothly. That can include life admin, errands, home and project coordination, travel planning, tech support, and discreet personal or family support. We tailor the scope to what you actually need.

How is this different from a normal personal assistant?

A traditional PA is often tied to a role or business. My focus is your whole life – work, home, family, projects and transitions. I work directly with you, not through a large agency, which keeps things personal, flexible and discreet.

Who is this service best suited for?

I typically work with business owners, senior professionals, returning expats, and families moving through busy or complex seasons. The common thread is not status – it’s responsibility. You’re holding a lot, and you’re ready to get proper support.

Where do you work?

I’m based in Ōtautahi Christchurch and work in person across Canterbury. Many tasks can be handled remotely, so I also support clients in Auckland, Wellington, Queenstown and elsewhere in New Zealand. Travel for specific projects or seasons can be arranged.

How do you handle privacy and confidentiality?

Discretion is central to this work. I don’t share client names, details or stories without explicit permission. For ongoing engagements, I’m happy to work under a written confidentiality agreement or NDA if you prefer.

How do your fees work?

Most clients choose a monthly retainer that gives them a set number of hours and priority access. For one-off projects, we can work on a fixed quote or an hourly rate. After our initial conversation, I’ll recommend a starting structure that fits your situation.
